# SRC and Cognitive Field Theory — Scope and Boundary

## What SRC Is

Self-Referential Closure (SRC) is the mathematical and structural layer published in this directory. It describes the conditions under which a self-referential system maintains a self-sustaining model of its own dynamics, formalized through two coupled equations tracking an external signal X and an observer description X̂. Closure stabilizes at an eigenform condition φ(s\*) = s\*: a fixed point where applying the description map to the system state returns the same state.

Structural properties — information threshold τ₁, variance ratio at the eigenform operating point, self-consistency of the correlation formula, and θ-invariance — are established results documented in `properties.md`. The same structural pattern appears independently in git branching discipline, blockchain state management, and molecular replication fidelity. SRC's theoretical lineage runs through Rosen's Closure to Efficient Causation, Pattee's Semantic Closure, and von Foerster and Kauffman's eigenform work.

## What CFT Is

Cognitive Field Theory (CFT) is the broader framework within which SRC is situated. CFT scaffolds SRC together with a set of foundational axioms about distinction, and motivates why the structure takes the form it does. SRC is the dynamical and structural component of CFT. CFT provides the theoretical scaffolding above it.

## Why CFT Is Internal

The foundational axioms of CFT and their downstream derivations are under active investigation. Publishing them alongside SRC would invite scrutiny the investigation is not yet positioned to meet, and would constrain iteration on the internal framework before the argument is fully assembled. This is sequencing discipline, not a refusal to publish. The published layer — SRC — is complete and self-contained on its own terms.

## What CFT Does That SRC Does Not

CFT does three things beyond what SRC provides.

**Foundational grounding.** CFT adds structural axioms about distinction as a primitive, situating SRC within a broader theory of cognitive substrate. SRC describes what closure looks like mathematically; CFT addresses why that structure is cognitively significant.

**Motivating the constants.** The constants τ₁ and a = 6 appear consistently across the instantiations documented in `properties.md` and `instantiations.md`. The empirical observation that they appear consistently is public. The motivation for why those specific values appear — rather than others — is part of CFT and is not published here.

**Scope extension.** CFT extends the reach of SRC claims beyond the structural parallels documented in this directory. The scope and status of those extensions are internal.

## What Is Published vs. Not Published Here

**Published in `research/src/`:**

- SRC dynamical equations (X, X̂, γ, κ, d)
- Eigenform condition φ(s\*) = s\*
- Structural properties as empirical observations: τ₁ threshold, variance ratio, self-consistency, θ-invariance
- Cross-domain instantiations: git branching (SRC-coherent branching, see `instantiations.md`), blockchain state management, Eigen's error catastrophe
- Grounding in Rosen, Pattee, von Foerster, and Kauffman

**Not published here:**

- CFT foundational axioms
- Derivations from those axioms
- The theoretical motivation for the values of τ₁ and a = 6
- Claims in domains beyond the instantiations documented in this directory

This separation is intentional. The published material is self-contained: the equations stand on their own, the properties are proven within the stated scope, and the cross-domain parallels are grounded in public literature. What is omitted is not a gap in the argument — it is the boundary of this publication.

## Forward-Looking Note

The relationship between SRC and CFT will be revisited as CFT material reaches publication-ready status. Until then, treat `research/src/` as a self-contained reference. Claims that CFT motivates but that are not documented here are not part of this publication and should not be inferred from it.
